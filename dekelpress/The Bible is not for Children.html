Jonah

Don't be an artist if you have any other choice.
Don't become a poet unless you've found your voice.
Certain jobs are not a living but they are a life
Which you create day by day with a brush and palette knife.

The poets once were prophets and they did a show and tell.
Ezekiel had to bake his bread on cow dung, with the smell,
Lying on one side for thirteen months; it was performance art.
Why would anyone volunteer to take on such a part?

Moses himself argued he was slow of speech and tongue.
Jeremiah begged off saying he was much too young.
Hosea was commanded to go take a whoring wife.
A prophet's job was not a picnic, not an easy life.

God told Jonah to go and preach to a certain city
If they turned from their wicked ways, then He would show them pity. Jonah didn't realize, it was an offer he couldn't refuse.
He tried to run, he tried to hide, he tried to take a snooze.

The long and short of the story goes, they throw him overboard
He gets swallowed by a big fish and then he seeks the Lord,
His words preserved in poetry, two thousand years and more.
The fish gets indigestion and spits him on the shore.

Sometimes you must get swallowed before you find your voice. Sometimes you think your destiny is strictly your own choice
There's many a slip twixt cup and lip especially for a fish.
Though choice and chance each play a part, be careful what you wish.
